How we work

The Majibu framework

Invest. Strengthen. Manage. A three-part approach designed to create the conditions for genuine, sustained, community-led change in East Africa.

The framework

Three interconnected commitments

Each element of the Majibu model reinforces the others. We don't simply disburse grants — we build long-term relationships with organisations that have the potential to become enduring pillars of their communities.

01

Invest Multi-year, unrestricted funding

Most philanthropic capital flows in short, restricted cycles that force NGOs into a perpetual scramble for survival. Majibu invests differently. We provide multi-year, largely unrestricted funding that enables partner organisations to plan strategically, attract and retain talent, and focus their energy on mission rather than fundraising.

Our due diligence process is rigorous but relationship-led. We look for organisations with strong local governance, demonstrated community trust, clear theory of change, and the ambition to build institutions that outlast individual leaders or funders — including Majibu itself.

Multi-year horizons Unrestricted core funding Relationship-led due diligence Local governance focus
02

Strengthen Technical support & capacity development

Funding alone is insufficient. Majibu embeds practical technical support alongside every investment, addressing the specific organisational development needs each partner identifies. This isn't generic capacity-building — it is targeted, responsive, and driven by what partners actually need to grow.

Support typically covers areas including financial systems and sustainability planning, digital data analysis and M&E frameworks, governance and board development, communications and philanthropic positioning, and programme design and evaluation. We draw on the expertise within our own team and a wider network of trusted specialists.

Financial sustainability planning M&E frameworks Governance support Digital data systems Board development
03

Manage Philanthropic stewardship & reporting

Majibu manages the full philanthropic relationship on behalf of our funding partners — from initial assessment and investment decisions through to ongoing oversight, reporting, and strategic review. For high-net-worth philanthropists who want genuine impact without the administrative burden, this is the core of what we offer.

We provide clear, qualitative and quantitative reporting that connects your investment to outcomes on the ground. We also ensure compliance with UK regulatory requirements, including CIC reporting obligations and our Donor Advised Fund arrangements with Chapel & York.

Full philanthropic management Impact reporting Regulatory compliance DAF structure

Our beliefs

Guiding principles

Local leadership is non-negotiable

We do not impose structures, strategies, or staff from outside. Every organisation we support is led by people from their own communities, with deep contextual knowledge that no external actor can replicate.

Trust is the foundation of impact

Long-term, respectful relationships — between funder and intermediary, and between intermediary and partner — are what allow genuine risk-taking, honest reporting, and adaptive management. We invest in trust first.

Durability over visibility

We are not in the business of creating photogenic projects. We are building systems, institutions, and capacities that will serve communities long after any funding has ended. Our partners are building legacies.

Rigour without bureaucracy

We take evidence and accountability seriously — but we know that excessive reporting requirements drain energy from the work. We design our monitoring frameworks to be genuinely useful to partners, not just reassuring to funders.
